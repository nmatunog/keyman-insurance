Module 1: Corporate Anatomy & Legal Structures

Philippine entity types create different continuity, liability, and estate-tax exposures. Advisors must map each structure before recommending funded solutions.

Sole Proprietorship

DTI-registered. No separate juridical personality — business debts reach personal assets. Death ends the enterprise unless heirs continue informally.

Risk: unlimited liability

Partnership

SEC or general partnership. Civil Code default: death of a partner dissolves the firm unless agreement says otherwise — triggers valuation and buyout pressure.

Risk: automatic dissolution

Corporation

Separate entity; shares are estate assets. Probate can freeze transfer of shares, blocking cap tables and lender covenants until estate tax is settled.

Risk: shareholder gridlock

The Three Hats Framework

Business Owner — continuity, lender confidence, funded buy-sell.

Discovery question: "If your partner died next quarter, who votes their shares?"

Employer — retention, deferred comp, key person.

Discovery question: "What revenue walks out the door if your top producer left?"

Family Head — TRAIN 6% estate tax, non-probate transfers.

Discovery question: "Where will heirs get cash to pay BIR without selling the company?"

Advanced: TRAIN Law estate freeze playbook

When a controlling shareholder dies, voting shares often cannot transfer until estate tax (6% over threshold) is paid in cash. Insurance owned by the estate or funded buy-sell proceeds can be structured to provide liquidity without fire-selling operating assets.

  • Map illiquid blocks on the personal balance sheet (land, unlisted shares).
  • Separate corporate continuity (buy-sell) from personal estate (irrevocable trust / personal policy).

Master Class case lab

Scenario: Family-held corporation, 3 siblings equal shares, ₱80M revenue, ₱12M net income. Father-founder still chairs board. Task: draft a 15-minute discovery agenda using Three Hats and identify which hat gets the first funded solution.

Module 2: Funding Buy-Sell Accords

Corporate-owned or cross-owned life insurance funds share purchase agreements without draining operating cash or triggering probate delays.

Cross-purchase

Each owner buys policy on others. Proceeds fund personal purchase of deceased's shares. Policy count: n×(n−1).

Best for 2–3 owners

Stock redemption

Corporation owns policies; proceeds redeem shares into treasury stock. Fewer policies to administer.

Best for 4+ shareholders

7-step buy-sell implementation

  1. Obtain latest financials and cap table
  2. Agree valuation method (formula, appraisal, fixed)
  3. Draft buy-sell agreement (lawyer)
  4. Select funding mechanism (cross vs redemption)
  5. Corporate resolution authorizing premiums
  6. Issue policies; assign to agreement
  7. Annual review with CPA on tax treatment

Advanced: Trigger events checklist

Standard agreements should define: death, disability (define % loss), retirement (optional), voluntary exit, bankruptcy, divorce (if shares marital). Each trigger needs a price formula and funding source.

Master: Board resolution language (sample)

"RESOLVED, that the Corporation is authorized to enter into a Stock Redemption Agreement and to purchase life insurance on shareholders [names] in amounts consistent with the attached valuation schedule, with the Corporation as owner and beneficiary of such policies."

Module 3: Key Person & Executive Retention

Quantify economic loss, align beneficiary design with BIR deductibility goals, and position corporate-owned cash value where appropriate.

Open the full Keyman Planning Reference (calculator, tests, discovery agenda)

BIR Section 36 — beneficiary drives everything

  • Corp is beneficiary: Death benefit generally exempt (Sec. 32); premiums NOT deductible (Sec. 36). Use for key person indemnification.
  • Employee/estate is beneficiary: Premiums may be deductible (Sec. 34); death benefit can flow to taxable estate. Use for golden handcuffs / DBO-style benefits.

Valuation methods (choose defensibly)

  • Salary multiple: 5–10× annual compensation (common for sales leaders)
  • Profit contribution: % of EBIT attributable × years to replace
  • Replacement cost: Recruiting + training + lost revenue window

Master: Objection — "Insurance is expense, not investment"

Reframe: "We're not buying an investment — we're buying a balance-sheet hedge against a named risk the auditors already worry about: sudden loss of [name]'s relationships and decisions. The question is whether you self-insure from retained earnings or transfer it for a known premium."

Module 4: Corporate Prospecting & Joint Ventures

Access HNW boardrooms through CPAs, corporate lawyers, and the Corporate Cash Risk Review.

"Mr. President, if your co-founder passed away tomorrow, their family may inherit voting shares under Civil Code rules. That forces you to drain cash, borrow, or accept inexperienced partners. I help boards install non-probate funding so you keep control and families get immediate liquidity."

Gatekeeper bypass channels

  • CPA: offer continuity checklist for audit season
  • Corporate counsel: co-branded shareholder risk memo
  • Bank RM: covenant review when key person uninsured

Master: 90-day JV pipeline

Week 1–2: 10 CPA intros. Week 3–4: 5 lunch-and-learns. Week 5–8: 3 joint client reviews. Week 9–12: 1 board presentation. Track referrals in CRM with source = "JV-CPA".

Prospecting tools expand at Basic; pipeline system at Master.

Membership & billing FAQ

Professional vs GIYA Elite?
Professional (₱999/mo) is the learning community — resources, monthly master class, and 20% off Academies. GIYA Elite (₱2,999/mo) is the full ecosystem — all Academies and coaching. GIYA Fellow is an earned designation — not a subscription.
What is a Legacy Consultant?
Advisors who complete Master Class academies may earn the Legacy Consultant designation — recognizing advanced case work in business insurance, estate, and succession planning.
Should I buy one Academy or join GIYA Elite?
One Academy is ₱7,990–₱12,990 (standalone). If you want more than one program, GIYA Elite at ₱2,999/month is often the better economics — plus coaching.
Is Discover the same as Preview?
Yes — free assessment, newsletter, and webinars. Upgrade to Professional for community benefits or purchase Academies.
If I cancel GIYA Elite after one month?
Elite benefits end when your paid period ends — access returns to Discover until you renew. A standalone Academy you purchased separately remains yours.
