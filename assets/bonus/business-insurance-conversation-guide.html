Business Insurance Conversation Guide

Opening scripts and objection bridges for advisors serving business owners — Keyman, buy-sell, and continuity.

Startup & family business

"Most owners built the firm around one or two people. If something happened to them tonight, would the business still run Monday morning — or would banks, clients, and staff all pause?"

Partnership / buy-sell

"If a partner died tomorrow, their family could inherit voting shares under default rules. I help structure funded buyouts so remaining partners keep control and the family receives agreed liquidity."

Common objections

ObjectionBridge
"We're too small."Smaller firms often have higher concentration risk — one person may be most of the value.
"We have savings."Savings are for growth; insurance creates instant, tax-efficient liquidity without draining working capital.
"Premiums are not deductible."Correct — we focus on continuity and tax-free proceeds to the corporation, not a tax deduction story.
"We'll do it later."Underwriting follows health today. Delay often costs more than starting with a defined key person amount.
